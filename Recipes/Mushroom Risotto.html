Mushroom Risotto

★★★★★

Guest Menu, Rice, Sides

Prep Time: 10min Cook Time: 30 min Servings: 2 Source: allrecipes.com

Ingredients

4 cups chicken broth

3 tablespoons salted butter

1/2 pound white mushrooms, thinly sliced

1/2 white onion

2/3 cups risotto rice

1/2 cup dry white wine

1/3 cup Parmesan cheese, grated

1 Flat Iron Steak

Directions

In a pot, warm the broth over low heat.

Melt 2 tablespoons butter in another large saucepan over medium-high heat.

Stir in the mushrooms, and cook until soft, about 3 minutes.

Remove mushrooms and their liquid, and set aside.

Add 1 tablespoon butter to skillet, and stir in the minced onion. Cook 1 minute.

Add rice, stirring to coat, about 2 minutes.

When the rice has taken on a pale, golden color, pour in wine, stirring constantly until the wine is fully absorbed.

Add 1/2 cup broth to the rice, and stir until the broth is absorbed.

Continue adding broth 1/2 cup at a time, stirring continuously, until the liquid is absorbed and the rice is al dente, about 15 to 20 minutes.

Remove from heat, and stir in mushrooms with their liquid, 1 tablespoon butter, and parmesan. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

Steak:
Melt 1 Tbsp butter and 1 Tbsp olive oil in a small frying pan. Cook on highest heat.

Once pan is hot, place steak in the pan and cook for 4 min. Do not move the steak around.

Next, flip the steak and cook for another 4 min.

Cut a small incision in the middle of the steak to see if it is cooked enough. If not, cook for another minute on each side.

Remove the steak and place under a foil tent to rest for a few min.

Cut into strips and and place on top.
