Papa Rellena

★★★★★

Beef, Leftover Friendly, Peruvian

Prep Time: 45 mins Cook Time: 25 min Total Time: 1 hr, 10 min Difficulty: Hard Servings: 2 servings (4 papas) Source: thespruceeats.com

Ingredients

3 eggs (save one for potatoes)

3 medium yellow potatoes

1/4 onion

2 cloves garlic

1 tablespoon aji pepper paste

1 tbsp cumin

1 tbsp paprika

1/2 pound ground beef

1/2 cup beef broth

1/2 cup flour

Description

The best dish in the world!

Directions

POTATOES:

Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the unpeeled potatoes until they are tender when pierced with a fork.

Once the potatoes are cooked, drain the water. Peel them, and then mash the potatoes thoroughly (or pass them through a potato ricer) until like a sticky dough.

Season the mashed potatoes with salt and pepper to taste.

Chill the potatoes uncovered for several hours, or overnight.

Once the potatoes are very cold and you are ready to form them, stir the reserved egg into the mashed potatoes until well-combined.

FILLING:

Hard boil all but one egg. Reserve 1 egg.

Sautee the onions, garlic, and aji pepper in the vegetable oil until soft and fragrant.

Add the cumin and paprika to the onions and cook 2 minutes more, stirring.

Add the ground beef and cook until browned. Drain some of the oil if excessive.

Add the beef broth and simmer for 10 to 15 minutes more, or until most of the liquid is gone.

Season mixture with salt and pepper to taste. Remove from heat and let cool.

Peel the hard-boiled egg and chop into small pieces before adding them to the filling mixture.

FORMING THE PAPAS:

With floured hands, place about 1/4 cup of mashed potatoes in one hand, and make a well in the center. Fill the well with 1-2 tablespoons of the beef mixture. Mold the potatoes around the beef, adding more potatoes if necessary to fully enclose the filling, and shape the whole thing into an oblong potato shape, with slightly pointy ends, about the size of a medium potato.

Repeat with the rest of the mashed potatoes. Coat each stuffed "potato" generously with flour.

In a deep skillet, heat 2 inches of oil on high heat. Fry the potatoes in batches until they are golden brown. Drain them on a plate lined with paper towels.

Notes

If using raisins and olives, add to the beef mixture once the broth has evaporated.
Best served with Peruvian Garlic White Rice recipe.
