Mushroom, Spinach, and Walnut Soup

★★★★

Leftover Friendly, Soup

Source: butimhungry.com

Ingredients

12 oz. button mushrooms, thinly sliced

4 tablespoons butter

2 garlic cloves, chopped

1/4 cup sherry (or white wine)

1/4 cup flour

4 cups chicken broth

1 teaspoons salt

pepper

2/3 cup finely chopped walnuts

1 cup heavy cream

3 cups baby spinach

Directions

In a large pot, melt the butter over medium-high heat. Add the mushrooms, stir well, and cook for approximately 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the mushrooms are nicely browned. Add the garlic and cook for another minute. Add the sherry, stirring until the mushrooms are dry again. Add the flour and cook for a minute, stirring constantly. Slowly add the chicken broth, stirring as you go. Stir in the salt, pepper, and walnuts and turn the heat down to low so that the soup is at a low simmer. Continue to simmer for another 10 minutes.

Add the cream, and let simmer for a few minutes until the soup begins to thicken. Then add the spinach and cook until just wilted, only a minute or two. Using an immersion blender, puree the soup until mostly smooth with some chunks still intact. (If you don't have an immersion blender, ladle half of the soup into a blender, puree well, and pour back into the pot.) Serve immediately.
