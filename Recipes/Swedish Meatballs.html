Swedish Meatballs

★★★★

Beef, Guest Menu, Leftover Friendly

Prep Time: 20 mins Cook Time: 10 mins Servings: Servings: 8 people Source: cafedelites.com

Ingredients

Meatballs:


1/4 cup fresh parsley

1/2 yellow onion

4 garlic cloves

1/2 pound 85% ground beef

1/3 cup breadcrumbs

1 egg

1 teaspoon salt

1 teaspoon of black pepper

1/4 teaspoon all spice

1 tablespoon butter

2 teaspoons olive oil


Gravy Sauce:

1/3 cup butter

1/4 cup all purpose flour

1 cup beef broth

1 cup milk

2 teaspoons worchestershire sauce

1 teaspoon dijon mustard

1/4 cup sour cream

Salt and pepper, to season

Directions

Pulse the onion, garlic, and parsley in the food processor until very fine.

Combine the ground beef, onion, parsley, garlic, spices, egg, and breadcrumbs.

Roll into 14 meatballs.

Heat 1 tablespoons of butter and 2 teaspoons of oil in a pan on medium-high heat. Fry meatballs in batches of 7. Transfer to a plate when done.

Add the 1/3 cup butter to the juices in the pan to melt. Whisk in the flour until it dissolves and turns into a roux.

Pour in the broth, milk, worchestire sauce and dijon. Bring to a to simmer and season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix the sauce through well to combine all of the flavours together.

Continue to simmer until thickened. Mix the meatballs into the sauce and serve.

Notes

*Replace heavy or thickened cream with reduced fat cream if you wish to reduce calories)

1) I use a mixture of ground beef and pork (mince) in may recipe, but you can use all beef is you don't have or like pork

2) To make the meatballs lower in carbs, use almond meal to replace the breadcrumbs. Also, use 1 tablespoon of cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons of water to replace the flour in the gravy sauce. Just add the cornstarch mixture at the end, while continuously stirring, over low heat until sauce has thickened.

3) I like to grate my garlic clove with the small part of a grater. You can use minced garlic if you prefer.
