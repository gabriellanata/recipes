Pasta with Huancaína Sauce

★★★★

Pasta, Peruvian, Vegetarian

Prep Time: 30 mins Cook Time: 20 mins Difficulty: Medium Servings: 2 Source: perudelights.com

Ingredients

Pasta for 2 people

1/3 yellow pepper (2 aji amarillo peppers)

2 tbsp vegetable oil

1/3 red onion

2 garlic cloves

1/4 cup milk

1/2 cup queso fresco

1/2 slice bread

1 tbsp aji amatillo paste

1 tbsp aji panca sauce

1 tbsp tari sauce

1 tbsp uchucuta


Salt

Aji amarillo powder

Parmesan cheese

Parsley leaves to garnish

Directions

Slice the yellow pepper in half, take out the seeds and veins, and chop.

Heat the vegetable oil in a skillet over medium heat. Add onion and garlic, and stir for about 4 minutes. Add the chopped pepper and cook for 3 extra minutes. Turn off the heat and cool.

In the medium sizdd food processor add the milk, queso fresco, bread, and salt. Process until very smooth, adding a little more milk if necessary.

Once the sauteed vegetables have cooled a bit, add and process as well.

Cook the pasta in a saucepan with salted boiling water.

In the meantime, transfer the huancaína sauce to a skillet and heat over medium heat. You may add extra milk if too thick.

Drain the pasta and combine with the huancaína sauce. Serve in four plates and sprinkle with Parmesan cheese. Garnish with parsley leaves.
